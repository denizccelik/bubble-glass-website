Our Vision

Rethinking
Glass for

We envision a future where glass — one of humanity's oldest materials — becomes the foundation of a fully circular, traceable supply chain for coastal and water-based environments. By closing the loop on glass production, we eliminate waste streams and create regenerative material flows that protect marine ecosystems while enabling a new generation of blue economy products.

The Challenge

A System
Built to Waste

🌊

Uncontrolled Waste Streams

Glass waste from coastal industries — fisheries, hospitality, naval — enters the environment with no tracking, no recovery, and no circularity. It accumulates in lagoons and seabeds with no infrastructure to recover it.

🔥

Energy & Water Intensive Processes

Conventional glass manufacturing is among the most energy-intensive industrial processes. High-temperature furnaces, excessive water consumption, and carbon-heavy logistics compound environmental costs across the entire chain.

⭕

No Circular Models for Water Ecosystems

Current recycling frameworks were built for land-based economies. Coastal and water-based environments lack dedicated circular models, traceability systems, or design standards for glass-derived materials that function in aquatic contexts.

Our Approach

A Circular
Glass Material
System

Bubble Glass designs a closed-loop material system that collects, processes, traces, and reintroduces glass waste as premium circular materials. Each fragment becomes a resource — tracked from origin to application.

  1. 01
    Collection & Sorting Glass waste is gathered from coastal and water-adjacent sources with full provenance logging.
  2. 02
    Low-Energy Processing Proprietary cold-processing and reduced-temperature methods minimize energy demand.
  3. 03
    Material Traceability Each material batch is digitally tagged, enabling full lifecycle transparency from source to application.
  4. 04
    Circular Reintroduction Processed glass re-enters the economy as certified modular components for architecture, design, and infrastructure.

Why Venice
"Venice is the world's most urgent laboratory for the intersection of water, material culture, and circular economy."

A City as a Pilot

Venice has a millennia-old relationship with glass craftsmanship — from Murano's furnaces to modern industrial production — creating both a rich material heritage and a significant waste legacy.

As a lagoon city under constant climate pressure, Venice faces the most acute version of the challenges coastal cities worldwide will encounter within decades, making it the ideal first test case.

Venice's unique governance structure, dense creative economy, and established design culture create the conditions for rapid prototyping and community-embedded material innovation.

Success in Venice becomes a replicable model for coastal cities from Amsterdam to Osaka — cities where water, waste, and design intersect in urgent and scalable ways.

Impact

Built to
Matter Beyond
the Product

Environmental

Protecting Coastal Ecosystems

By diverting glass waste from landfills and waterways, Bubble Glass directly reduces marine contamination. Circular processes lower carbon emissions and cut industrial water consumption across the production chain.

Cultural

Preserving Material Heritage

Venice's glass tradition is a living cultural heritage. By reframing it through circular principles, we ensure this craft legacy continues — not as nostalgia, but as relevant contemporary practice with global meaning.

Economic

New Value Chains for Coastal Cities

Circular glass materials create new categories of traceable, premium products — supporting local industries, attracting blue economy investment, and generating models exportable to coastal economies worldwide.

Scalability

The Bubble Glass system is designed for replication. Once proven in Venice, the model expands to coastal cities sharing similar pressures: marine waste, industrial glass use, and urgent need for circular infrastructure.
